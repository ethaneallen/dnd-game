================================================================================
                    D&D 5e WEB GAME - PLAYER'S GUIDE
================================================================================

Welcome, adventurer! This guide will help you navigate the world of this 
D&D 5th Edition web-based game.

================================================================================
                           GETTING STARTED
================================================================================

1. Open index.html in your web browser to start the game
2. Create a new character by selecting:
   - Race (Human, Elf, Dwarf, Halfling, Half-Orc, Tiefling, Dragonborn)
   - Class (Fighter, Wizard, Rogue, Cleric, Ranger, Barbarian)
   - Background (Soldier, Scholar, Criminal, Noble, Outlander, Acolyte)
3. Choose a campaign to play
4. Your stats are rolled automatically using the 4d6-drop-lowest method
5. Your character's personality (trait, ideal, bond, flaw) is generated

================================================================================
                           GAMEPLAY
================================================================================

HOW THE GAME WORKS:
  The game has TWO main actions for progressing:
  
  🗺️ TRAVEL - Moves you to a NEW LOCATION
     - This is how you advance the STORY
     - Story events trigger when you arrive at key locations
     - Look at the green HINT below your objective!
     - Example: "💡 Use TRAVEL to go to the Keep Gates"
  
  🔍 EXPLORE - Searches your CURRENT location
     - Random encounters (combat, treasure, events)
     - Used for grinding XP and gold
     - Some story events require exploring (check the hint!)
     - Example: "💡 EXPLORE and defeat 5 goblins"
  
  The green hint below your objective tells you exactly what to do!

EXPLORATION:
  - Click "Explore" to search your current location
  - Random events include combat, treasure, merchants, and story events
  - Skill check events test your abilities for rewards
  - Different location types have different dangers:
    * Towns (🏠) - Safe, have shops, low danger
    * Wilderness (🌲) - Moderate danger, travel areas
    * Dungeons (💀) - High danger, best loot

COMBAT:
  - Initiative determines who acts first (DEX modifier + d20)
  - Attack: Roll to hit vs enemy AC, deal weapon damage
  - Defend: Gain +2 AC for the round
  - Spell: Cast spells (casters only)
  - Flee: Attempt to escape (DEX check vs DC 12)
  - Critical Hits (Natural 20): Trigger special effects!
  - Fumbles (Natural 1): Bad things happen!
  - Companions attack automatically after your turn

REST:
  - Short Rest (1 hour): Spend hit dice to heal, limited uses
  - Long Rest (8 hours): Full HP restore, reset abilities
    * Inn: Safe, costs 5 gold
    * Camping: Free but 30% chance of interruption

TRAVEL:
  - Move between locations using the Travel button
  - Use the Mini-Map for quick navigation
  - New locations unlock as you progress through chapters
  - Story events trigger at key locations
  - Travel advances time and may change weather

================================================================================
                           CHARACTER SYSTEMS
================================================================================

STATS (Abilities):
  - STR: Melee attacks, carrying capacity, Athletics
  - DEX: Ranged attacks, AC, initiative, Stealth/Acrobatics
  - CON: Hit points, Constitution saves
  - INT: Wizard spells, Investigation, Arcana
  - WIS: Cleric/Ranger spells, Perception, Insight
  - CHA: Social skills, Persuasion, Intimidation

PERSONALITY:
  - Trait: Your character's quirk or habit
  - Ideal: What drives you (Greater Good, Freedom, Honor, etc.)
  - Bond: What you're connected to
  - Flaw: Your weakness (may trigger complications!)

INSPIRATION:
  - Earned through great roleplay moments
  - Spend for advantage on any roll
  - Can only hold one at a time
  - View via ✨ Status button

EXHAUSTION (6 Levels):
  1. Disadvantage on ability checks
  2. Speed halved
  3. Disadvantage on attacks and saves
  4. HP maximum halved
  5. Speed reduced to 0
  6. DEATH
  - Remove with long rest or Recuperating downtime

CONDITIONS:
  - Poisoned: Disadvantage on attacks
  - Frightened: Disadvantage on ability checks/attacks
  - Paralyzed: Can't move or act, auto-crit if hit
  - Blinded: Disadvantage on attacks, advantage against you
  - Prone: Melee advantage, ranged disadvantage against you
  - Restrained: Speed 0, disadvantage on attacks
  - Stunned: Can't act, attackers have advantage

================================================================================
                           PARTY SYSTEM
================================================================================

RECRUITING COMPANIONS:
  - Meet potential companions during story events
  - Unlock them by completing specific objectives
  - Access party management via 👥 Party button
  - Maximum party size: 4 (including you)

COMPANION MECHANICS:
  - Each has their own HP, AC, stats, and weapon
  - Attack automatically during combat each round
  - May be targeted by enemies (40% chance per enemy turn)
  - Can fall unconscious if HP reaches 0
  - Unconscious companions cannot attack

COMBAT PARTICIPATION:
  - Companions attack after your turn in combat
  - Their attacks use their weapon and stat modifiers
  - Critical hits and fumbles apply to companion attacks too
  - Party status is displayed during combat showing all companion HP
  - Enemies intelligently choose targets between you and companions

HEALING COMPANIONS:
  - Open the Party panel (👥 button) to heal companions
  - Use Potion of Healing (2d4+2 HP) on wounded companions
  - Use Potion of Greater Healing (4d4+4 HP) for more healing
  - Can revive unconscious companions with potions!
  - Healing companions increases their loyalty
  - Reviving unconscious companions gives +10 loyalty bonus

LOYALTY SYSTEM:
  - Ranges from -100 (Hostile) to +100 (Devoted)
  - Affected by your decisions and treatment
  - Healing companions increases loyalty (+3, or +10 for revival)
  - Companions falling in combat decreases loyalty (-5)
  - Below -50: Companion deserts!
  - Character flaws may reduce starting loyalty

COMPANION LIST:

================================================================================
                           EQUIPMENT
================================================================================

WEAPONS:
  Your equipped weapon determines your attack damage.
  
  Weapon Properties:
  - Finesse: Can use DEX instead of STR for attack/damage
  - Versatile: Higher damage when used two-handed
  - Two-Handed: Cannot use with a shield
  - Thrown: Can be thrown for ranged attacks
  - Ranged: Uses DEX, attacks from distance
  - Light: Good for dual-wielding
  - Heavy: Best for strong characters
  - Reach: Attack from further away

ARMOR:
  - Light Armor: Add full DEX modifier to AC
  - Medium Armor: Add DEX modifier (max +2) to AC
  - Heavy Armor: No DEX modifier, highest base AC
  - Shields: Add +2 or +3 to AC (cannot use with two-handed weapons)

GETTING NEW EQUIPMENT:
  1. SHOPS: Visit shops in towns to buy equipment
  2. ENEMY LOOT: Defeated enemies drop gold and sometimes equipment
  3. TREASURE CHESTS: Found while exploring, may be trapped!
  4. BOSS DROPS: Bosses always drop rare equipment
  5. CRAFTING: Create items from monster materials

Campaign-specific items are marked with ⭐ and have an orange highlight.
Click the ℹ️ button on any item to see detailed stats.

SPELLCASTING CLASSES:
  - Wizard (INT): Fire Bolt, Magic Missile, Shield, Scorching Ray
  - Cleric (WIS): Sacred Flame, Cure Wounds, Guiding Bolt, Bless
  - Ranger (WIS): Hunter's Mark (bonus damage to marked target)

SPELL TYPES:
  - Cantrips: Unlimited use, scale with level
  - Level 1 Spells: Use spell slots, restore on long rest
  - Level 2 Spells: More powerful, unlock at higher levels

SPELL CATEGORIES:
  - Damage: Fire Bolt, Magic Missile, Scorching Ray
  - Healing: Cure Wounds
  - Buff: Shield (+5 AC), Bless (+1d4 to attacks)
  - Control: Hold Person (paralyze enemy)

CONCENTRATION:
  - Some spells require concentration to maintain
  - Taking damage requires CON save (DC 10 or half damage)
  - Failing ends the spell effect
  - Can only concentrate on one spell at a time

SPELL SAVE DC: 8 + proficiency + casting stat modifier

================================================================================
                           COMBAT SPECIAL RULES
================================================================================

CRITICAL HITS (Natural 20):
  Roll on the critical hit table for bonus effects:
  - Devastating Blow: Double all damage dice
  - Stunning Strike: Enemy is stunned
  - Disarming Strike: Enemy drops weapon
  - Precise Hit: Extra 1d6 damage
  - Brutal Critical: Max die + normal roll
  - Momentum Strike: Gain bonus attack!

FUMBLES (Natural 1):
  Roll on the fumble table for penalties:
  - Dropped Weapon: Must retrieve next turn
  - Off Balance: Enemy gets advantage
  - Self Injury: Take 1d4 damage
  - Wide Open: -2 AC until next turn
  - Fumbled Footing: Fall prone
  - Embarrassing Miss: -1 to next attack

DEATH SAVING THROWS:
  At 0 HP, you're dying (not dead yet!)
  - Each turn, roll d20
  - 10+ = Success, under 10 = Failure
  - 3 Successes = Stabilize
  - 3 Failures = Death
  - Natural 20 = Wake up with 1 HP!
  - Natural 1 = Two failures
  - Taking damage = automatic failure

MULTI-ATTACK:
  Fighters, Barbarians, and Rangers at level 5+
  Make TWO attacks per Attack action

================================================================================
                           TROUBLESHOOTING
================================================================================

Game won't load:
  - Make sure JavaScript is enabled in your browser
  - Try a different browser (Chrome, Firefox, Edge recommended)
  - Clear browser cache and reload

Save files missing:
  - Saves are stored in browser local storage
  - Private/Incognito mode does not persist saves
  - Clearing browser data removes saves

Combat seems unfair:
  - Check your equipped weapon and armor
  - Make sure you're not fighting enemies above your level
  - Use the Defend action to reduce damage taken
  - Flee and rest if your HP is low
  - Recruit companions for help!
